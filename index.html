    From Awareness to Action

    What We Do

    Three interlocking pillars address a different link in the chain between contaminated water and preventable illness.

    Community Health Workshops

    Teaching families to recognize, prevent, and respond to water-borne illnesses like cholera, typhoid, and dysentery — with safe storage and hygiene practices that outlast any single visit.

    EducationHygiene

    Water Treatment Kits

    Portable, affordable purification kits distributed directly to households drawing water from unprotected dams, rivers, and open wells — an immediate safeguard while infrastructure catches up.

    PurificationHouseholds

    Borehole Advocacy

    Lobbying and partnering with NGOs and local government to fund mechanized boreholes — sustainable water sources that reduce long-term dependency on treatment kits altogether.

    InfrastructurePartnerships

    Seven in Ten, Ten in Time

    Within five years, 7 out of every 10 families in the rural communities we serve across remote Africa will have reliable access to quality drinking water.
